        LUNCH REVIEW
        I came here for a late business lunch of 10 people and had a great experience! My co-workers were very happy with the choice, and we were able to sample a lot of the lunch menu.

        CHARCUTERIE
        The charcuterie boards for lunch are unfortunately preset, and you cannot customize them. There were perfectly crispy baguette slices that came on the boards, and you can ask your waiter for more bread if you have a large party.
        Salty Pig Board (+): Definitely the better board to order. The baguette perfectly complemented the pork shoulder and smoked pork pieces. The Stracciatella cheese was flavorful and a really big hit with our table.
        Cured Board: I did not enjoy the jar of meat that came with the board. There was a layer of cold fat coating the top of it.

        SALAD
        Baby Kale Salad: The salad was overpowered by the large pear pieces and dressing.
        Escarole Salad (+): This was the better option of the two. There were salami pieces, hard boiled egg, and cheese chunks that gave the salad a variety of flavors and textures. Definitely choose this salad as a starter!

        PIZZA
        Peperonata (+) This was the best pizza of the three that we had tried. The sausage was rich, flavorful, and salty, perfectly mixing with the chunks of goat cheese that were cooked in each slice.
        Margherita: A classic pizza to accomodate those who felt like they were eating too much meat. Nothing special to report here, besides the fact that the pizzas were cooked very well - crispy on the crust without being dry, and moist with structure towards the center of the pie.
        The Salty Pig: I was kind of disappointed with this pizza, since it was called "Salty Pig pizza" and seems like this would be the pizza that they're known for. There was various cuts of meat that basically tasted like cold cuts that were warmed with arugula sprinkled on top of it. Although there were mustard and pale ale caramel incorporated into the pizza, I really couldn't taste it.

        BEER
        Get the coffee beer! For $4.50, this beer was very unique in that it was rich from the coffee infusion without being heavy as a beer.

        The Salty Pig is OK. I've enjoyed the place each time for its food and menu, but it deserves 3 out of 5 stars simply because its service is subpar.

        Other Yelpers have made a point about the service. The service is mostly inattentive and slow. You generally need to chase down your server for food and drink requests. Nonetheless, the food is quite good.

        The menu is divided into four main parts: bread dishes with cheese and meat (delicious!), appetizers, pasta, and pizzas. We had the venison cacciatorini bread entree with herbes de Provence and dried mushroom. The flavors were rich and I especially loved how they added some sweetness in the dish with some jam.

        Usually, the assortments of meats are quite impressive. The meats are usually combined with cheeses, jams, fruits, and spreads. The real fun in the bread entrees is playing around with your selection of bread add-ons including the fig jam, mushroom conserva, jalapeño jelly, and smoked shallot marmalade. It's interactive, fun, and social.

        Seriously, I would recommend taking advantage of their meat selection and choosing adventurous dishes including the roasted bone marrow and the pork tasting. Choose the dishes which sound like they have a good balance of savoriness and sweetness - the two tend to go well together! The pizzas are also affordable and filling.

        Lastly, I would recommend going with a friend rather than a date. The slow service can certainly dull and irritate your mood especially if it's a busy night or the weekend. Certainly, tryout the Salty Pig because their food is affordable and pleasant, but the the service is somewhat disappointing for such a rockstar menu.

        Listed in Bawston

        I've read someone postulate that across America, any restaurant with the word "pig" in the name is delicious to justify any and all pigging out--I've found this to hold true, and the Salty Pig just adds further proof.  Although I'm not a huge fan of cured meats (I've always found them fatty, I learned from a toxicology that nitrates and nitrites are bad for you, and that salami often gives you a funny breath), the Salty Pig was the restaurant I was most excited about while planning my trip to Boston--it seemed to have an edgy originality and swanky classiness, and during my visit it exceeded my expectations.

        Just like any twentysomething tourist n00bie foodie wannabe who is rather clueless on gourmet cured meats and cheeses, we asked our server for recommendations, who was helpful in pointing out the popular no-fail items, offering detailed descriptions, describing portion sizes, and suggesting wine pairings.  This is the photogenic and memorable deliciousness we ended up with:

        Prosciutto di Parma, ITA aged 16 Months ($8): These thin slices are so delicately complex, it almost puts bacon to shame.

        Chimay a la Biere, Trappist, Belgium, cow, washed rind, soft & strong ($7): For some reason it tasted exactly as you would expect a "Chimay" to be, bold but nothing exotically bizarre.  (Yeah... I have a very limited and inaccurate jargon for describing cheese)

        Smoked Shallot Marmalade ($2): I know onion jam sounds strange, but it added a nice dimension, balancing savory with sweet.

        Complimentary charcuterie board items: I like the grainy mustard, and the pickles are good too.  The Crostini bread is mind-blowing... even though it's the type of crusty bread that's tougher to chew, the bite-size pieces are perfect, especially for putting everything on top (not entirely sure this is how you're supposed to eat high-end cured meats, but it is so delicious that I cannot describe it)

        Margherita Pizza, Tomato, Mozzarella, Basil ($13), + arugula ($1.5): I am usually not a big fan of margherita pizza because it's basic to the point of being boring, like the plain pizzas you used to get for lunch at elementary school.  But the pizzas here are pretty on point, with the fresh puffy crust and all, just like at Italian restaurants that claim authentic secret-family-recipe pizzas. The arugula was great on the pizza and partially disguised it to be along the healthy lines of a salad.  I'm sure adding the egg would have been delicious because that's what eggs do to everything, but we had already splurged enough for the night to warrant another topping.

        We were classy and got zinfandel, but personally I think beer would have gone great with Salty Pig fare.

        The ambiance was expected: to use the same words as I did above, it had indeed edgy originality and swanky classiness, rustic like BBQ but dressed up ultra fancy. Think shadowy spotlight lighting, badass graffiti art, lamps that look like dynamite.

        It got crowded when we went on a weekday night, but you are likely to get seated quickly if you have a small party and don't mind the communal table.

        It came out to be quiet expensive especially with the a la carte charcuterie board, but if you are visiting Boston and have an absolute love for food, the Salty Pig will likely leave you with no regrets, no matter how unhealthy and salty and pigging out it sounds.

        This was my first time coming here without reading any Yelp reviews. I had no idea what to expect and our waitress laid everything out for us. We listened to her advice and ordered the charcuterie, followed by an entree.

        Listed in Yelp 100 2014, Favesies

        Slightly tucked away, the Salty Pig is the wet dream of every carnivore out there - leave your vegetarian friends at home. You can create your own charcuterie plate, and there's an option to add extra prosciutto or even a fried egg to any pizza. The place is swimming in arugula, cured meats, and good beer - basically, heaven.

        The staff is extremely friendly and chatty, and the ambiance is pretty loud - there's definitely a bit of a bar atmosphere, with the televisions and the extensive chalkboards. If you're in the mood for anything that doesn't involve cured meats or pizza, your pickings aren't bad either - they've got some pasta selections, a polenta with egg and procini, and (gasp!) a lone kale salad. For the more adventurous, you can get beef heart on your pizza, or head cheese on your charcuterie plate.

        Definitely come here early, because the line and the wait gets beyond ridiculous during the prime hours.

        I dedicate this review to our wonderful server, Christy, who's humor and enthusiasm really made our date night special.

        I'm not really a pork parts enthusiast. My boyfriend, Jason L, however, lives for the stuff, so I agreed to accompany him to the Salty Pig, with the promise of a pizza compromise.

        First things first, they do not take reservations for parties less than 6. It is a small space and there isn't really a lot of place to idle while waiting for your table.  We went on a Monday night around 8pm and were told it was a 45 minute wait. The hostess took our name and number. We were granted access to an app that allowed us to see how many patrons were ahead of us, and were told that they'd text us when our table became available. I LOVED this accommodation. It allowed Jason L and I a more civilized waiting experience. We happily skipped across the street and had a drink. Table was free within 45 minutes, text came promptly and we were seated immediately.

        We ordered:

        1) Prosciutto di Parma, Aged 16 Months: $8
        2) Porchetta, Pork Shoulder, Fennel Pollen, Rosemary: $7
        3)Manchego de Corcuera, Sheep, Aged 3 Months, Rich & Buttery: $6
        4)Bianco Sardo, Sardenia, Sheep, Aged 6 Months, Sharp: $7
        5) Marinated Olives: $3
        6) Marcona Almonds: $3
        7) Fig Jam: $2
        8) Pizza Bianca, Speck, Brussel Sprouts, Provolone, Sunnyside Egg: $16

        Verdict: WAY TOO MUCH FOOD! We didn't need the pizza, the meat and cheese board filled us up, but damn I'm happy we got it anyway. We were both very happy with the price point, the meat and cheese servings were large and delicious. The olives are addicting- I wouldn't judge you if you ordered two servings of these bad boys. The almonds are buttery. The meat and cheeses are served with huge helpings of perfectly crispy, buttery, lovingly charred bread. You will want to eat this bread on its own. Don't do it. Smother it with cheesy goodness. Fig jam tastes awesome with the sharp cheeses. YADA YADA meat and cheese perfection...

        But OH!, the PIZZA! Dear god. The Pizza Bianca was the best pizza i've ever had, AND I LIVED IN NEW YORK CITY. It was salty, it was savory, it was melty, and cheesy and it was  cooked to perfection.  If I could eat it every night of the week. I want it in my belly right now. SIGH. The only reason why I'm not eating it right now is 1) Salty Pig is not currently open and 2) I live in Quincy and it's hard to get to on the regular. I literally hate all the people that live within walking distance of this pizza.... but I digress...

        Space was warm, intimate, dark but not depressingly so. The small bar space was bustling and lively. Food came promptly. Christy, our before mentioned server was awesome! She cheered us on as we ordered, and congratulated us on our "exquisite taste." She checked on us frequently and explained all of our choices to us and suggested pairings. She did everything right in terms of service, but really what made her special was her warmth and enthusiasm. At the end of the night, Christy surprised us with a free desert and thanked US for being so nice!? We were completely shocked and a little embarrassed. Shouldn't we be thanking her for being so great? Anyways, it was some sort of chocolate thing, and it was delicious, obviously.

        I think I can safely speak for Jason L on this one, we are definitely going back!

        P.S. Get the "Bear Skin Rug" cocktail.... and repeat after me Rum Chata!

      Specialties

      Located on the end of the beautiful Southwest Corridor with a patio overlooking a gorgeous Boston skyline, The Salty Pig is a new restaurant from the people behind Coda Bar and Kitchen and Canary Square. We feature charcuterie, pizza, American craft beer and unique imported selections. We open at 4pm daily. Late night dining until 12:30pm nightly. Lunch begins July 25th.

      History

      Established in 2011.

      We are the fourth restaurant in the group including Canary Square and Coda bar and Kitchen. They formally owned the Common Ground Bar and Grill in Allston for 16 years.
